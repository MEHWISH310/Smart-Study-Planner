Time Management

Master the art of time management with proven techniques, tools, and strategies. Learn how to prioritize tasks, avoid procrastination, and make the most of your study sessions.

Time Management Techniques

Pomodoro Technique

Break your work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

  1. Choose a task to work on
  2. Set a timer for 25 minutes
  3. Work on the task until the timer rings
  4. Take a short break (5 minutes)
  5. After four pomodoros, take a longer break (15-30 minutes)
Tip: Use our built-in timer below to practice this technique!

Eisenhower Matrix

Prioritize tasks by urgency and importance, sorting out less urgent and important tasks.

  1. Create a 2x2 matrix with "Urgent/Not Urgent" and "Important/Not Important"
  2. Place your tasks in the appropriate quadrant
  3. Focus on tasks that are both urgent and important first
  4. Schedule time for important but not urgent tasks
  5. Delegate or eliminate tasks that are not important
Tip: This helps prevent the "tyranny of the urgent" where urgent tasks dominate your time.

Time Blocking

Plan out every moment of your day in advance by dedicating specific time "blocks" for certain activities.

  1. Divide your day into blocks of time (30 minutes to 2 hours)
  2. Assign specific tasks or activities to each block
  3. Include blocks for breaks, meals, and personal time
  4. Stick to your schedule as closely as possible
  5. Review and adjust your blocks weekly
Tip: Color-code your blocks to easily distinguish between different types of activities.

Eat That Frog

Tackle your most challenging task first thing in the morning when your energy and willpower are highest.

  1. Identify your most important task (your "frog")
  2. Do this task first thing in the morning
  3. Don't spend too much time thinking about it - just start
  4. Reward yourself after completing the task
  5. Make this a daily habit
Tip: If you have two frogs, eat the ugliest one first - tackle the hardest task first.

80/20 Rule (Pareto Principle)

Focus on the 20% of tasks that will yield 80% of the results.

  1. Identify all the tasks you need to complete
  2. Determine which tasks will have the biggest impact
  3. Prioritize these high-impact tasks
  4. Spend less time on low-impact activities
  5. Regularly reassess which tasks are high-impact
Tip: This principle applies to many areas - 20% of your study topics likely cover 80% of exam content.

Getting Things Done (GTD)

A five-step method for organizing and tracking tasks to reduce mental clutter and increase productivity.

  1. Capture: Collect all your tasks and ideas in an inbox
  2. Clarify: Process each item and decide what needs to be done
  3. Organize: Sort tasks into categories and priorities
  4. Reflect: Regularly review your system and tasks
  5. Engage: Actually do the tasks
Tip: The key is to get everything out of your head and into a trusted system.
